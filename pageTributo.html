Lee Teng-hui
The True Father of Taiwanese Democracy
Today, Taiwanese citizens choose their leader every four years through direct election. If it were not for the actions of Lee Teng-hui when he was in office, this might not be the case. As president, Lee pushed to end the state of martial law and reinstate elections for leaders of the Republic of China. It is because of these courageous decisions that I think of Lee as the true father of Taiwanese democracy.
Some details about Lee's life:
  • Born in a small farming community in Japanese Formosa (modern Taiwan) - 1923
  • Volunteered service in the Japanese Army during the Second World War
  • Graduated from Kyoto University - 1946
  • Graduated from National Taiwan Univeristy (bachelor's degree in agricultural science) - 1948
  • Graduated from Iowa State University (master's degree in agricultural economics) - 1953
  • Returned to Taiwan to modernize Taiwan's agricultural infrastructure - 1957
  • Graduated from Cornell University (doctorate degree in agricultural economics) - 1968
  • Joined the Chinese Nationalist Party, or Kuomintang (KMT - 國民黨) - 1971
  • Began service as a minister in the KMT government- 1972
  • Began service as mayor of Taipei - 1978
  • Began service as governor of Taiwan Province - 1981
  • Began service as vice president of the Republic of China - 1984
  • Began service as president of the Republic of China and chairman of the KMT - 1988
  • Promised protesters that full democracy was coming - 1990 March 21
  • Pushed to end martial law and eliminate the Temporary Provisions Effective During the Period of Communist Rebellion - 1991
  • Became the first elected president of the Republic of China - 1996
  • Oversaw the first transition of power to rival political party, with Democratic Progressive Party (DPP) leader Chen Shui-bian taking over the office of president - 2000
After leaving office in 2000, Lee went on to support the pan-green coalition led by the DPP, and oppose candidates in favor of Chinese unification from his former party. For these reasons, I consider Lee a true patriot of Taiwan, and one of the most important figures in its history.
